Introduction to Communication

Syllabus

Tense and types of tenses, The Sequence of Tenses (Rules in use of Tenses) and Exercises on it. Introduction to Phonetics: Introduction, Phonetic Transcription, English Pronunciation, Pronunciation Guidelines Related to consonants and vowels, Sounds Mispronounced, Silent and Non-silent Letters, Syllables and Structure, Word Accent and Stress Shift, – Rules for Word Accent, Intonation – purposes of intonation, Spelling Rules and Words often Misspelled – Exercises on it. Common Errors in Pronunciation. Question Tags, Question Tags for Assertive Sentences (Statements) – Some Exceptions in Question Tags and Exercises, Useful Practices of Grammar Articles and Prepositions, Subject-verb agreement, noun-pronoun agreement, Oral Communication Listening skills, Public speaking, impromptu speaking, Situational dialogues

Teaching Methodology

The sessions will be conducted in both lecture-style teaching and interactive, activity-based learning. The emphasis will be more on activities.

Faculty

  • prof. Ms. Preeti Shukla

Learning Outcome

  • Grasp and implement the foundational principles of communication within their interactions.
  • Think freely, recall the topics taught in class and implement them.
  • Engage in conversations using grammatically accurate English, while also incorporating nuanced phonetics, vocabulary, intonation, and improved pronunciation which is free of MTI.
  • Public speaking skills.

Additional Reference

Study and exercise material which is available on the internet for free, will be used with appropriate changes. Relevant videos (TED Talks, BBC, movies) will be used for training.

Assessment Structure

  • Continuous Assessment : 20 Marks
  • Internal Assessment Tests (IA) : 30 Marks
  • End-Term Exam : 50 Marks
